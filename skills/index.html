Skills

These skills are specific to our Star Trek HERO game, but anything from the HERO book can be used if it makes sense.

General Skills

Systems Operation has been broken up into separate skills with a slightly lower cost. Some of them are also Everyman skills, but those only allow basic use. For example, every character can enter a set of coordinates into a transporter and activate it. However, they would need real skill to choose a location on their own or to deal with any problems which may arise.

Engineering Skills

Astronautics, Mechanics and Electronics are general skills in this game. Skill levels must be purchased separately for individual fields, but they apply for all three general skills. For example, if you buy a skill level in Sensor Systems Technology as a skill level, you can apply it to Electronics for repairing a bridge console, or for Mechanics if you're repairing a satellite dish. You could also use it for Astronautics if you're working on the ships sensor array.

Engineering Cost Stat Type Description
Astronautics 3 INT Skill Starship Engineering
Electronics 3 INT Skill General skill with electronics, including computers
Mechanics 3 INT Skill General skill for mechanical devices

Skill Level Costs

Skill levels are purchased normally, but top out at 5 points because there are only seven technology skills and that's not enough to justify 8 or 10 points. You can buy a 2-point level several times to be a specialist, or cover more ground with a larger cost.

Cost Description
2 +1 with with single technology
3 +1 with any three related technologies
5 +1 with the whole group of technologies

Military Skills

Taking a skill level in Starfleet unarmed combat doesn't necessarily reveal a person as Starfleet personnel (although it could if the observer is an expert) but there's a martial arts package which is very distinctive. Choosing specific Starfleet maneuvers will broadcast to others where you were trained.

Everyman Skills

Skills aren't needed for things like Acting, Conversation or Concealment unless you want to be good at them. Even a familiarity indicates an above average proficiency.

This list assumes that the characters are in the Triangle, two years after the Four Years War with the Klingons. (Phasers are relatively new, so laser weapons are the most common weapon.) Players are free to take one, none or all of these skills.

Universal Translator devices repeat aloud like real-life translators at the United Nations, so it's far better to speak the same language as another person. Everyone is assumed to speak their native language and Galacta for free, without even putting them on a character sheet. Galacta is a trade language that's spoken everywhere, and is the lame excuse for why everybody spoke English in the show.
